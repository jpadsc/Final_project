DSC 106 final project by Javier Ponce

Have you ever woken up feeling worse than before you went to bed? If you said yes, you might have a bad sleeping posture. In the following project, I use visualizations to explain key ideas of how to sleep better while trying to find the overall best sleeping position.

Bad news for stomach sleepers

If you are like me and enjoy sleeping in your stomach, then I have bad news for you. The conductors of the experiment I pulled my data from decided to not include data for subjects sleeping on their stomachs. But even worse, stomach sleeping is widely considered to be the worst sleeping position so it is reasonable to ignore it when trying to find the best sleeping position. If you are a stomach sleeper, I hope this project encourages you to try some new sleeping positions, just like it encouraged me.

First visualization

In the visualization below we explore how different sleeping positions affect the distribution of pressure over the body of the subjects. The visualization allows you to navigate between 8 subjects and 16 sleeping positions, once a configuration is chosen you see the pressure distributed over the mattress (and the subjects body), highliting with blue the points with the highest pressure.



Takeaways

While exploring this visualization, you should be able to notice that all individuals registered less pressure when they were centered compared to when they were near the end of the bed or with a diagonal orientation. Another easy-to-infer finding is that heavier subjects do not necessarily exert more pressure. In reality, what matters the most is the comparison between their mass (weight) and the area of their side touching the mattress. For the same reason, we see a lot more blue when subjects sleep sideways since it reduces their area touching the mattress by half.

Pillow talk: Let's talk about pillows

Do the findings from the first visualization imply that sleeping on your side is bad? Not at all. Side sleeping has many benefits. The only thing that our previous visualization showed is that side sleeping is worse for distributing your weight around your body. Thankfully for us, pillows exist.

Second visualization

In this visualization, we explore how using supporting pillows can improve side sleeping. The visualization uses an interactive interface similar to the one from the first visualization. The main difference being that we only consider side sleeping and we select between different pillow configurations rather than sleeping positions.



Takeaways

The main takeaway is that pillows and sleeping wedges can significatlly improve side sleeping and what better way to show this than a visualization.

From the inclined plane to an inclined bed

Everyone who has taken a physics class knows that inclination matters when considering the force between an object and the inclined surface. Then, Could we use inclination to improve sleeping?



Takeaways

While the inclination is helpful in multiple situations, like trying to stimulate blood circulation, this visualization shows that inclination is not as helpful when trying to distribute pressure over the mattress. In fact, too much inclination seems to be bad for pressure distribution, showing significantly more blue for each subject.
